### HOGWARTS COMMONS

#### great_hall

```yaml
description: >
  The Great Hall stretches before you, impossibly long, its four house tables
  scarred with hex-burns and littered with abandoned goblets. Hundreds of
  candles float overhead in uneven clusters, their flames guttering in a wind
  that has no source. The enchanted ceiling churns with bruise-colored storms
  that never break — lightning crawls between clouds but no thunder follows.
  The air smells of cold stone and something faintly metallic, like the
  aftertaste of a spell gone wrong.

short_description: >
  The Great Hall is dim and unsettled. Candles gutter overhead and the
  enchanted ceiling writhes with silent, unnatural storms.

first_visit_text: >
  You open your eyes to candlelight and the distant rumble of magic gone
  wrong. The Great Hall surrounds you — but something is deeply off. The
  enchanted ceiling doesn't show the sky anymore. It shows a storm that
  belongs to no season, no geography, no world you recognize. Professor
  McGonagall stands at the head table, her expression carved from flint. Ron
  Weasley sits nearby, pale, picking at the edge of his sleeve. Neither of
  them looks surprised to see you. They look like they've been waiting.
```

#### entrance_hall

```yaml
description: >
  The Entrance Hall rises around you in cold marble, its vaulted ceiling lost
  in shadow. The grand staircase dominates the north wall, its flights
  shifting with grinding, arrhythmic lurches — stairs swinging to landings
  they were never meant to reach. Portraits line every surface, their
  occupants huddled in clusters, whispering behind painted hands. The floor
  tiles are cracked in a radial pattern, as though something struck the
  center with tremendous force and no one bothered to repair it.

short_description: >
  The marble Entrance Hall echoes with the grinding of shifting staircases
  and the nervous whispers of a hundred portraits.

first_visit_text: >
  You step into the Entrance Hall and the grand staircase lurches — an
  entire flight of stone swings sideways with a groan that vibrates in your
  teeth, connecting to a landing three stories above where it has no business
  going. The portraits gasp in unison, a sound like rustling paper. One of
  them — a medieval witch in a starched ruff — catches your eye and shakes
  her head slowly. "Don't trust the stairs, dear," she whispers. "They've
  forgotten where they belong."
```

```yaml

  covered in a hand you cannot quite focus on, as if the ink resists being
```

#### seventh_floor_corridor

```yaml
description: >
  The seventh-floor corridor is quieter than the rest of the castle — an
  almost reverent hush, broken only by the distant grinding of staircases
  below. The left wall is conspicuously blank: smooth, unbroken stone where
  every other corridor in Hogwarts is cluttered with portraits and tapestries.
  The blankness has a quality to it, a sense of potential, as if the wall is
  listening. Opposite, a tapestry of Barnabas the Barmy being clubbed by
  trolls hangs slightly askew. Scorch marks on the floor tiles suggest
  recent experimentation.

short_description: >
  The seventh-floor corridor is hushed. One wall is conspicuously blank and
  seems to listen. Scorch marks dot the floor.
```

#### courtyard

```yaml
description: >
  The open courtyard offers the first breath of outside air, and it tastes
  wrong — thin and metallic, like licking a copper Knut. The sky overhead is
  a deep, burnt amber that belongs to no hour of the day, casting everything
  in sepia tones. A stone sundial stands at the center, its gnomon snapped
  clean off, and the shadow it no longer casts would be pointing in an
  impossible direction anyway. Cloistered walkways frame the space on three
  sides, their arches casting geometric shadows across flagstones scattered
  with dead leaves that crunch like parchment.

short_description: >
  The courtyard sits under an amber sky that belongs to no natural hour. A
  broken sundial stands at its center, gnomon snapped clean.
```

#### greenhouse

```yaml
description: >
  Glass walls and ceiling panels let in the sickly amber light, refracting
  it into prismatic bands across rows of potting benches and hanging baskets.
  The greenhouse is a riot of magical botany — Venomous Tentaculas strain
  against their pots, puffapods quiver at your approach, and a Whomping
  Willow sapling thrashes in a reinforced planter. The air is thick, humid,
  and sweet with the smell of dragon dung fertilizer and fresh-turned earth.
  Somewhere in the back, something is growing at a rate visible to the naked
  eye, its tendrils creeping along the glass.

short_description: >
  The greenhouse is humid and overgrown with magical plants straining
```

#### clock_tower_base

```yaml
description: >
  The base of the clock tower is a stone chamber dominated by the massive
  clock mechanism overhead — a cathedral of brass gears, iron pendulums, and
  crystal escapements, all frozen mid-motion. The great clock has stopped.
  Its hands are visible through a gap in the ceiling, fixed at an hour that
  doesn't correspond to any number on the face. A thick layer of brass dust
  coats every surface, disturbed only by a single set of footprints leading
  to the mechanism and back. The jammed works groan occasionally, as if
  trying to move against some invisible restraint.

short_description: >
  The clock tower's massive mechanism is frozen mid-motion, its gears
  locked. Brass dust coats everything. The clock face shows an impossible hour.
```

```yaml
  hum at slightly different frequencies, creating a dissonant chord that
  vibrates in your chest. Moving through the maze means navigating by
  history rather than direction.
```
